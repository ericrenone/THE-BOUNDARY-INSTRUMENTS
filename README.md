**CRUCIBLE-X** · *Quantum Vacuum Birefringence Polarimeter*

The vacuum, threaded by a magnetic field B, acquires a refractive index anisotropy Δn = (2α²/45π)(B/B_crit)² ≈ 4.0×10⁻²⁴ · (B/T)². This is the primary signature that the vacuum's col(F)/ker(F) boundary is mode-dependent: the two photon polarization eigenstates propagate at different speeds because each probes a different projection of the QED Lagrangian correction. CRUCIBLE-X combines a focused XFEL probe pulse (17.5 keV, 10¹² photons/shot, 25-fs duration at European XFEL) with a co-focused petawatt optical pump laser (10 PW, HIBEF undulator beamline, Helmholtz International Beamline for Extreme Fields). At the crossed focal region—field strength ~10¹³ T equivalent—a differential polarization rotation of Δθ ~ 10⁻¹⁰ rad accumulates. Downstream, a crossed analyzer pair with noise floor below this angle reads the signal. The machine reads one number: the mode-splitting of the vacuum, which is the projection of the Euler-Heisenberg Lagrangian onto the observable polarimetry algebra.

**VEIL-SPEC** · *Vacuum Dichroism Ellipsometer*

Vacuum dichroism is the imaginary part of the refractive index split: Im(Δn). Where birefringence rotates the polarization ellipse, dichroism differentially absorbs the two modes, changing the ellipticity. In a strong crossed-field geometry, the two effects co-exist with calculable ratio, so an ellipsometric extension of CRUCIBLE-X—adding a Stokes-parameter analyzer rather than a single crossed polarizer—extracts both Re(Δn) and Im(Δn) simultaneously. VEIL-SPEC inserts a four-channel polarimetric detector downstream of the CRUCIBLE-X focal region, measuring all four Stokes parameters at the X-ray probe wavelength with shot-by-shot normalization. The dichroism measurement constrains the imaginary part of the Euler-Heisenberg effective action, providing an independent handle on the vacuum's absorption boundary.
